Oval Kerf Offset — Help

Beam dimensions

The first three boxes define the shape of your laser beam. Cut a test octagon and measure it across the width, height, and diagonal with calipers, then enter those dimensions in millimetres into the boxes.

e.g. A 20mm octagon that measures height 19.95mm, width 19.91mm, diagonal 19.925mm. Subtract each measurement from 20mm to get half the beam size (the radius), then multiply by 2 to get the full beam size: 0.1mm, 0.18mm, 0.15mm.

Curve resolution & degrees

These two boxes control how many nodes are plotted on your offset lines. The defaults work well in most situations — one node per millimetre on gentle curves, one node per 10 degrees on sharper curves. This gives good accuracy with minimal file size. For extreme accuracy set Curve Resolution to 0.3.

Importing a cut path

The Drop SVG / Click to Browse zone is self-explanatory.

Merge nodes slider

A hidden slider appears when there are nodes closer than 0.5mm in your SVG. This lets you merge very close nodes that could cause small loops or spikes in the offset path.

Trace Kerf Offset

Traces inner and outer kerf offsets for your SVG shapes.

Remove Inner Corner Loops

Removes loops in straight line corners.

Remove Curve Loops

Removes loops caused by extremely sharp curves.

Sharp Corners

Gives an accurate kerf offset — e.g. for the points on stars.

Blunt Corners

For shapes that need to be inlaid. A star with accurate sharp points won't fit into its pocket (the hole your shape fits into) because the pocket's points can only be as sharp as your laser beam width. Blunt Corners removes the tip of each point so your inlay fits perfectly.

Sharp and Blunt Corners only work with straight lines.

Downloading

Use the tick boxes to choose which paths to include, then download your offset SVG.

Beam Presets

Open the file in a text editor. The preset section is at the very top of the code, clearly marked LASER BEAM PRESETS — EDIT YOUR LASER SIZES HERE. Each entry has a name, width, height, and diagonal — edit these to match your machines, add new entries, or remove ones you don't need. Save the file when done. Do not edit anything outside this section or you may break the code.
